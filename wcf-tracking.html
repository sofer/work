Notes

To add tracking for files, simply place **tracking.js** inside the head tags at any point after loading jquery as follows:

<script src="<path-to-js-files>/tracking.js" type="text/javascript"></script>

The list of file paths is on the first line of the tracking.js file and can be amended. Use lower case only in the definitions (although the files themselves can be upper or mixed case, too). pdf, ppt, doc, docx, pptx, xls and xlsx are all currently recognised.

Any links outside the current host will be ignored for tracking purposes.

tracking.js relies on the presence of jquery and the GA library. It looks like there is a bit of namespace conflict between jQuery and mootools, so we are using $jpcc in place of $.

We have also moved the GA code--which is invoked asynchronously--to the bottom of the header and added a call to <ga_social_tracking.js>.

At present, the Facebook 'like' button has been embedded synchronously (it is also generating the warning: "FB.getLoginStatus() called before calling FB.init(). "). It is possible that you may want to change this in future, but for now a call to _ga.trackFacebook(); can added just before the closing <body> tag. See also these notes on adding facebook to a page.

Meanwhile, from the Google developers website: "Google Analytics provides integrated reporting for the Google+ Badge and +1 Button. This means if you have implemented ga.js and either a +1 Button or Google+ Badge on the same page, all +1 and Badge social interactions will be automatically reported". See more here.

We have not implemented the twitter or linkedin tracking yet, but recommend that somebody implements the changes outlined above first and can confirm that everything is working as expected before proceeding further.

Vimeo integration may be a bit trickier. Vimeo is usually loaded in an iframe. Tracking content embedded on a third-party site in an iframe is not trivial. There are workarounds, such as using an in-page image that require a user to click in order to view the video, or embedding additional in-page controls. We will hold off doing more work on this until we know more about the approach that is going to be used in this case.

Saving Lives

Over a quarter of a million women and three million newborn babies die each year in pregnancy and childbirth or soon afterwards, the majority of them in Africa and South Asia. For every woman who dies at least twenty more suffer complications which leave them with lifelong disability and pain.

Our unique programmes are saving the lives of mothers and babies every day. We need you to help us to equip women with their most vital survival tool: knowledge.
